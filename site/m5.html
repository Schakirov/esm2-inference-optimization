Milestone 5 · torch.compile & static shapes

Does graph compilation help ESM2 encoder inference on the L4 — and what does it cost? The harness measures the two numbers people conflate separately: cold start (first call at a shape, includes compilation) and steady state (CUDA-event-timed after warmup). Result: torch.compile gives a real 1.3×–2.2× steady-state speedup — largest on the small overhead-bound shape — at a ~28 s per-shape compile cost that recurs for every new shape under static shapes.

What the benchmark says

Key takeaways

  • Compile wins everywhere, most on the small shape. Every compiled cell beats eager. The biggest win is the overhead-bound seq 128 / batch 1 (2.15–2.20×): eager there is pinned on the ~20 ms launch floor, and fusion + CUDA graphs cut exactly that. The compute-bound seq 512 / batch 8 still gains 1.63×, so it is real kernel improvement, not only launch savings.
  • reduce-overhead ≈ default. CUDA graphs edge out plain Inductor only marginally (2.20× vs 2.15×, 1.64× vs 1.63×) — not a separate tier of win for this model on this GPU.
  • Cold start is ~28 s per shape, and it recurs. With dynamic=False every distinct (batch, seq_len) recompiles. The 512/8 cell saves ~87 ms/call → breaks even after ~340 calls; the 128/1 cell saves ~11 ms/call → needs ~2,500 calls.
  • Memory is essentially unchanged (~1.22–1.39 GB), in line with eager.
Honest framing

The cold-start asterisk

"2.2× faster" — but only after you pay for it

The steady-state speedups are genuine, but the headline cannot drop the cold-start cost. Under static shapes that ~28 s compile is paid per shape, and a variable-length protein workload hits many shapes. To make compile practical you would compile with dynamic=True (fewer recompiles, often a smaller per-shape win), bucket sequence lengths to a few fixed shapes, or pre-warm the compile cache for the shapes you serve. Compile pays off for high-volume, shape-stable serving — not one-off or highly shape-diverse runs.
