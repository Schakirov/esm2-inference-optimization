Milestone 7 · Profiling notes

torch.profiler on the encoder forward (bf16, seq 512, batch 8) gives a kernel-level breakdown whose self-times sum to 225 ms/iter — matching the Milestone 2 latency, which proves the attribution is honest. Time splits almost evenly between matmul (~42%) and unfused elementwise (~43%) — and that elementwise share is exactly the headroom torch.compile fused. The profile explains the compile win.

What was built

Kernel-level profiler

  • scripts/06_profile_pytorch.py — runs the forward under torch.profiler and reports a kernel-only view: leaf CUDA kernels (no aten:: ops), so GPU time is not double-counted and the self-times sum to the true forward latency. Kernels are rolled up into coarse categories.
  • docs/PROFILING.md — the full write-up (method, breakdown, limitations).
  • Only the small top-ops CSV is committed; the full Chrome trace is optional (--trace) and never committed.
The double-counting trap

key_averages() lists both operator events and the kernels they launch. Summing both read ~449 ms/iter — almost exactly 2× the real forward. Filtering to leaf kernels makes the total equal the measured 225 ms/iter; that equality is the proof the percentages are right.

What the profile says

Key takeaways

  • Attribution checks out. Leaf-kernel self-times sum to 225 ms/iter, equal to the Milestone 2 median — the forward runs essentially serially on one stream.
  • Attention is not the bottleneck here. The fused flash-attention kernel is only ~4.7%; the matmul cost is the FFN/projection linears (24.9% + 12.6%). Optimizing attention further would barely move this shape.
  • ~43% is unfused elementwise — the fusion headroom. GeLU, bias-adds, residual adds, and scalings each launch their own kernel and stream activations through HBM. That is exactly what torch.compile fuses, and it explains the 1.63× compile speedup at this same shape in Milestone 5.
  • ~14% is copies/casts (rotary cat, dtype casts); LayerNorm ~1%. Memory is ~1.4 GB of 24 GB — not capacity-bound.
Honest scope

Limitations

Read the buckets as order-of-magnitude

The category rollup is heuristic (kernel-name substrings): the matmul-vs-rest split is robust, but "elementwise" bundles activation/bias/residual/scale and "copy" bundles casts and concatenations. It is eager-only, one shape (seq 512 × batch 8), on this L4 + PyTorch 2.12.0+cu130 stack — a compiled run, a different shape, or another GPU gives a materially different split.
